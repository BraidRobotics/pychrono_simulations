Structural Efficiency vs. Weight

Purpose: Compares the structural efficiency of different designs by measuring how much force each can support relative to its own weight.
Calculation: Specific Load Capacity = Force at 10% compression / (Weight × g) where g = 9.81 m/s²
Interpretation: Higher values are better - they indicate the structure can support more load per unit of its own weight, meaning more efficient use of material. A value of 5.0 means the structure can support 5× its own weight.
