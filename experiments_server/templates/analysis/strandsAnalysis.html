Stiffness vs Compression

Purpose: Reveals the non-linear spring behavior of braided structures.
Key Insight: Unlike linear springs (F = kΔx with constant k), braided structures show varying stiffness:

  • Progressive Stiffening: Stiffness increases at low-to-medium compression (braid geometry locks up)
  • Softening Phase: Stiffness decreases at high compression (approaching buckling)

Interpretation: The peak stiffness point indicates optimal operating range before structural degradation begins.

Force vs Displacement (Non-Linear Spring Comparison)

Purpose: Directly compares experimental behavior to ideal linear springs (Hooke's Law: F = kΔx).
Visualization:

  • Solid lines: Experimental data from simulations
  • Dashed lines: Hypothetical linear spring with initial stiffness k₀

Key Observation: Experimental curves deviate from linear (dashed) lines, showing:

  • Concave up (stiffening) in early compression
  • Concave down (softening) in late compression

Conclusion: These structures behave as non-linear springs with variable stiffness k(x), not simple Hooke's Law springs.

  • Linear (F ∝ n): Material quantity dominates (axial compression)
  • Quadratic (F ∝ n²): Structural stiffness dominates (bending/buckling)

Interpretation: Linear scaling suggests simple material addition; quadratic suggests improved structural geometry.
