Maximum Survivable Load vs Thickness (All Data)

Purpose: Shows the complete structural strength envelope across all thicknesses.
Data: Maximum force each thickness configuration survived before structural failure (explosion).
Key Insight: This uses ALL experiment data (not just those reaching 10% compression), revealing the full scaling relationship.

Theoretical Models:

  • Linear (F ∝ t): Pure axial compression failure
  • Quadratic (F ∝ t²): Bending-dominated failure, cross-sectional area scaling
  • Quartic (F ∝ t⁴): Euler buckling from beam theory (Pcr = π²EI/(KL)², where I ∝ r⁴)

Interpretation: The data closely follows quartic scaling, indicating your braided structure behaves as a classic Euler column with buckling-dominated failure.

Structural Efficiency Scaling

Purpose: Shows how efficiently structures support load relative to their weight as thickness increases.
Question Answered: Should you use thicker strands or more strands to maximize load per unit weight?
Theoretical Models:

  • Constant (E ∝ t⁰): Efficiency unchanged with thickness (axial-dominated)
  • Linear (E ∝ t): Efficiency increases linearly (bending-dominated, F ∝ t², W ∝ t)
  • Cubic (E ∝ t³): Efficiency increases rapidly (buckling-dominated, F ∝ t⁴, W ∝ t)

Interpretation: If data follows cubic scaling, thicker strands are significantly more weight-efficient than thinner ones.
